The evolution of love in Arabic-Islamic literature

تطوّرُ الحبّ في الأدبِ العربي-الإسلامي

Emna Ben Ameur | Supervision: Nicolas Baumard, Valentin Thouzeau

Project in brief

Love is among the oldest words in Arabic literature, and one of the most layered. It speaks of passion and devotion, but also of knowledge, ethics, and the self.

This project studies how these meanings travel across centuries and genres, from classical poetry to theological prose. Using computational tools as reading companions, we look for continuity and transformation in how Arabic texts express love.

Beyond numbers or models, the goal is to understand how emotion takes form in language, and how, even through data, the human voice remains.
